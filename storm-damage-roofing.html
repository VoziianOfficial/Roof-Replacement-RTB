When This Category Fits

Situations that often align with weather-related roofing requests.

Storm damage roofing requests apply when weather events — including hail, high winds, lightning, heavy precipitation, or falling debris — have visibly affected your roof's condition. These weather-triggered issues often appear suddenly and may require rapid assessment and urgent repair or replacement. Storm damage can range from minor cosmetic impacts to structural compromise, and documenting the damage promptly helps with insurance claims and contractor communication. Our platform ensures that weather-related roofing concerns are properly categorized so you can connect with contractors experienced in rapid-response, damage-assessment, and weather-related repairs or replacements. The storm damage category acknowledges the time-sensitive nature of these situations.

What To Prepare

Useful information before submitting a storm-related request.

Storm damage requests require time-sensitive documentation and clear description of what changed. Photograph any visible damage while it's still fresh, document the date and time of the weather event, note the severity of weather (hail size, wind speed, or other details if known), and list all areas affected. Include information about whether you're dealing with active leaks, structural concerns, or cosmetic damage. Keep records of any insurance communication you've initiated. The faster and more detailed your documentation, the better contractors can prioritize assessment and provide estimates for emergency repairs or replacement.
